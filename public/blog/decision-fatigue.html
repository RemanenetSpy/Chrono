Decision Fatigue and the Evening Collapse

By The Archivist   •   Reading Time: 3 min

Every day, you make thousands of decisions. What to wear, how to reply to that email, what to eat for lunch, how to handle a crisis at work. By 8 PM, your prefrontal cortex—the part of your brain responsible for willpower and decision-making—is running on fumes.

This is decision fatigue. And it's the exact reason why, at the end of the day, you can't even make the simple decision to go to sleep.

Instead, you choose the path of least resistance: the infinite scroll. It requires zero decisions. The algorithm decides for you.

The Power of Constraints

When you are fatigued, open-ended tasks are paralyzing. "Journaling" sounds exhausting. "Meditating" feels like work.

The antidote to decision fatigue is a rigid, microscopic constraint. You don't need to write a page. You don't need to reflect on your life goals. You just need to write one sentence.

Chronos is designed around this exact constraint. It removes the burden of choice. One sentence. Once a day. It's the lowest possible barrier to entry, but it's enough to break the hypnotic trance of the algorithm and put you back in the driver's seat.
